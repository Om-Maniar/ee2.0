Formulae Used in Transformer Losses Analyzer

Losses and Efficiency Formulae

Eddy Current Losses

Eddy Current Losses are caused by induced currents in the core due to changing magnetic fields. The formula used is:

\( P_{\text{eddy}} = k_{\text{eddy}} \times f^2 \times B^2 \times V \)

Where:

  • \( P_{\text{eddy}} \): Eddy Current Loss (W)
  • \( k_{\text{eddy}} \): Core constant (0.0005 for CRGO, 0.0008 for Ferrite)
  • \( f \): Frequency (Hz)
  • \( B \): Magnetic Field (Tesla)
  • \( V \): Core Volume (m³)

Hysteresis Losses

Hysteresis Losses occur due to the reversal of magnetic domains in the core. The formula used is:

\( P_{\text{hyst}} = k_{\text{hyst}} \times f \times B^{1.6} \times V \)

Where:

  • \( P_{\text{hyst}} \): Hysteresis Loss (W)
  • \( k_{\text{hyst}} \): Core constant (0.001 for CRGO, 0.0012 for Ferrite)
  • \( f \): Frequency (Hz)
  • \( B \): Magnetic Field (Tesla)
  • \( V \): Core Volume (m³)

Copper Losses

Copper Losses are due to the resistance in the transformer windings. The formula used is:

\( P_{\text{copper}} = I^2 \times R_{\text{temp}} \)

Where \( R_{\text{temp}} = R \times (1 + \alpha \times (T - 20)) \)

And \( I = \frac{\text{Rated Power} \times \text{Load Percentage}}{\text{Secondary Voltage}} \)

Where:

  • \( P_{\text{copper}} \): Copper Loss (W)
  • \( I \): Current (A)
  • \( R_{\text{temp}} \): Resistance adjusted for temperature (Ω)
  • \( R \): Winding Resistance at 20°C (Ω)
  • \( \alpha \): Temperature coefficient (0.00393 for copper)
  • \( T \): Temperature (°C)

Stray Losses

Stray Losses are caused by leakage fluxes in conductive parts. The formula used is:

\( P_{\text{stray}} = 0.1 \times (P_{\text{eddy}} + P_{\text{hyst}} + P_{\text{copper}}) \)

Where:

  • \( P_{\text{stray}} \): Stray Loss (W)
  • \( P_{\text{eddy}} \): Eddy Current Loss (W)
  • \( P_{\text{hyst}} \): Hysteresis Loss (W)
  • \( P_{\text{copper}} \): Copper Loss (W)

Dielectric Losses

Dielectric Losses occur due to energy dissipation in the insulation material. The formula used is:

\( P_{\text{dielectric}} = V_{\text{primary}}^2 \times f \times k_{\text{dielectric}} \)

Where:

  • \( P_{\text{dielectric}} \): Dielectric Loss (W)
  • \( V_{\text{primary}} \): Primary Voltage (V)
  • \( f \): Frequency (Hz)
  • \( k_{\text{dielectric}} \): Dielectric constant (0.0001)

Total Losses

Total Losses are the sum of all individual losses. The formula used is:

\( P_{\text{total}} = P_{\text{eddy}} + P_{\text{hyst}} + P_{\text{copper}} + P_{\text{stray}} + P_{\text{dielectric}} \)

Where:

  • \( P_{\text{total}} \): Total Loss (W)

Efficiency

Efficiency is the ratio of output power to input power, expressed as a percentage. The formula used is:

\( \eta = \left( \frac{P_{\text{output}}}{P_{\text{input}}} \right) \times 100 \)

Where \( P_{\text{output}} = \text{Rated Power} \times \text{Load Percentage} \)

And \( P_{\text{input}} = P_{\text{output}} + P_{\text{total}} \)

Where:

  • \( \eta \): Efficiency (%)
  • \( P_{\text{output}} \): Output Power (W)
  • \( P_{\text{input}} \): Input Power (W)
  • \( P_{\text{total}} \): Total Loss (W)
